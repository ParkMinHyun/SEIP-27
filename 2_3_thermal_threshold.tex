\subsection{A Thermal Threshold Cannot Separate Safe and Unsafe Captures}
\label{sec:guard-limit}

The safeguard initially disabled optional Draft stages at thermal level~5 and
above. It was later tightened to level~4 after a Filter-related Capture Timeout
on a lower-tier model. Because the camera framework shares this policy across
models, the stricter threshold applies to flagship devices as well.

Table~\ref{tab:timeout_index} characterizes the limitation on a
current-generation flagship. For this experiment, we bypassed the deployed
guard and forced either Bokeh alone or Bokeh with Filter throughout 30-shot
portrait bursts. We varied thermal level, output resolution, and memory
pressure, and recorded the first capture that timed out.

The boundary fails in both directions. It is \emph{under-protective} below
level~4: Bokeh with Filter first times out at capture 29 under normal 24MP
capture even at level~0, and at capture 24 under normal 12MP capture at
level~2. Memory pressure moves failures earlier; for example, at level~3 the
first 24MP Bokeh-plus-Filter timeout moves from capture 12 to capture 5. It is
also \emph{over-restrictive} at and above the boundary. At level~4, the normal
12MP traces complete the first nine captures with Bokeh and Filter and the
first 15 with Bokeh alone, yet the deployed rule disables those stages from
the first capture.

These measurements do not imply that the early captures at level~4 are always
safe. Rather, they show that thermal level is not a sufficient decision
variable: the outcome at one level changes with resolution, memory pressure,
selected stages, and the backlog inherited from previous shots. Thermal state
is useful context for estimating execution time, but no nontrivial scalar
threshold in Table~\ref{tab:timeout_index} separates safe and unsafe captures.

\subsection{Limitations of Static Thermal Gating}
\label{sec:guard-limit}

\smallskip\noindent\textbf{Production thermal guard.}
During one model's pre-release validation, thermal throttling at overheat level 4 delayed a single-frame Draft stage and caused a Capture Timeout.
When the timeout recurred despite workload reduction and code optimization, we chose a uniform policy that disables all optional image processing for Draft Sequences starting at overheat level 4 or above.
Because the shared Draft control path has no model-specific branch, the same policy applies across models.
High ambient temperature, charging, and recent device workloads can readily raise the level to 4.
However, Samsung's overheat level indicates device temperature and throttling severity, not whether a capture's remaining deadline budget can cover its queued and remaining Draft work.

\smallskip\noindent\textbf{Target workload and setup.}
We denote the lightweight multi-frame Draft stage developed as a production candidate by \emph{M} and the deployed single-frame stage by \emph{S}.
The target Portrait Draft Sequence runs optional M and S before mandatory encoding and saving.
Our goal is to enable M across all supported device models and the full overheat range rather than disable it categorically at level 4.
To characterize the always-on risk even on our highest-performing model, we evaluated M on the S948U.
With M forced and the guard bypassed, we ran ten 30-capture trials per combination of starting overheat level (0--6), resolution, memory condition, and Draft Sequence configuration.
In the level-4 validation failure, the Draft backlog reached 12 sequences near capture 20.
A 30-capture horizon therefore covered this state conservatively.
Memory-pressure trials ran the camera alongside AnTuTu Benchmark~11.1.4's memory-stress workload.

Capture Timeouts occurred in forced-on trials that began below the level-4 cutoff.
At starting levels 4--6, M+S first timed out at captures 4--10 after several initial captures completed, although the production guard would disable their optional stages.
Thus, starting overheat level alone did not distinguish captures that completed M+S from those that timed out.

\smallskip\noindent\textbf{Required runtime control.}
Achieving this goal requires coordinating per-capture admission with capture pacing.
Admission compares each capture's remaining budget with the predicted cost of its complete remaining Draft Sequence.
However, admission acts only when a capture reaches the Draft worker, so it cannot recover budget consumed by the cross-shot waiting in Section~\ref{sec:parallel-capture} or prevent newly accepted captures from extending the backlog.
Pacing therefore delays \texttt{captureAvailable} according to predicted queued cost so the backlog can drain before another capture is accepted.
This delay can lengthen the earliest next-shot interval, making responsiveness an explicit trade-off.

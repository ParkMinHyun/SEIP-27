\subsection{Limitations of Static Thermal Gating}
\label{sec:guard-limit}

\smallskip\noindent\textbf{Production thermal guard.}
The Draft control logic in Samsung's camera framework is shared across device variants.
Galaxy Overheat Level is a device-temperature-based throttling state; higher levels impose stronger throttling and can lengthen the same Draft workload.
To contain this deadline risk, the framework disables all optional Draft image-processing stages from Level 4, where the existing single-frame stage \emph{S} had caused Capture Timeouts during production validation.
Level 4 occurs during repeated-capture and stress-validation workloads and can also arise from high ambient temperatures, charging, and preceding device workloads.
This deterministic rule is inexpensive to validate, but it does not estimate whether the current capture can complete within its remaining deadline budget.

\smallskip\noindent\textbf{Target workload and setup.}
We denote the lightweight multi-frame Draft stage introduced in Section~\ref{sec:draft-background} by \emph{M} and the existing single-frame stage by \emph{S}.
We evaluate M alone to isolate its cost and \emph{M+S} to represent their coexistence in Portrait mode.
We use the S948U, a high-capability flagship whose parallel-capture path has no additional queue-length restriction.
An earlier Capture Timeout incident on a constrained flagship involved 12 pending Draft Sequences and prompted a model-specific queue-length limit on parallel capture.
The S948U has no such cap, so we use 30-capture bursts as a common sustained-load horizon; the resulting pending count and worker wait vary with resolution and memory condition.
For each starting level (0--6), resolution, memory condition, and Draft configuration, we ran ten bursts in a validation build that forced the optional stages to execute by disabling the guard's conditional check.
For memory-pressure runs, the camera executed concurrently with AnTuTu Benchmark's memory-stress workload.

The fixed cutoff does not account for capture-specific deadline risk.
In 24MP memory-pressure bursts begun at Level 3, M+S timed out as early as capture 7.
Across the tested configurations, the earliest observed failure shifted with resolution, memory pressure, enabled stages, and burst position.
The common cutoff therefore lacks the remaining-budget and queued-cost information needed to decide whether M can execute on a particular capture.

\smallskip\noindent\textbf{Required runtime control.}
These results motivate deciding whether to execute M from each capture's remaining budget and predicted Draft cost.
Admission alone, however, cannot recover budget already consumed while waiting behind earlier Draft Sequences.
We therefore coordinate admission with capture pacing based on predicted queued cost rather than a fixed queue count.
Pacing can reduce future queueing, but any delay to \texttt{captureAvailable} increases the earliest next-shot interval.
